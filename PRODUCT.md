# MarketPulse — Product brief

## What it is
A focused stock-tracking app: watchlists, portfolio, charts, and an AI assistant that answers questions about your holdings. Single-user. No social features, no trading.

## Register
**brand** — this site is a portfolio piece for the developer (Abhishek). The marketing surface (Homepage) is the product. The app surfaces (Dashboard, Portfolio, Analytics, Assistant) are utility. Treat the Homepage as design-IS-the-product, the rest as design-SERVES-the-product.

## Users
- **Primary**: technical recruiters and freelance leads evaluating Abhishek's frontend craft. They land on the Homepage, glance at the demo, and decide whether to read the case study.
- **Secondary**: a hypothetical retail trader using the app — informs UX heuristics but is NOT the audience the visual design has to win over.

## Tone
- **Confident, not loud.** A portfolio piece for senior eyes; over-decoration reads as inexperience.
- **Editorial, not corporate.** Closer to a Bloomberg longform than a Bloomberg terminal.
- **Numbers respected.** Prices, percentages, and timestamps deserve typographic weight; throwaway labels don't.

## Anti-references (designs to avoid)
- **Robinhood/Cash App vibe** — bright accent on white, oversized friendly type, bold cartoon emoji. Wrong tone for a craft showcase.
- **Crypto exchange** — neon-on-black, monospace everything, flashing tickers. Conveys gambling.
- **AI SaaS landing template** — gradient orbs, "Master the Market" headlines, identical 6-feature card grids, fake testimonials. The Homepage shipped with all of these; we are deliberately replacing every cliché.
- **Bloomberg terminal pastiche** — green-on-black, dense data, no whitespace. Fine for traders, wrong for "look at my craft."

## Strategic principles
1. **Restraint over decoration.** Empty space is doing work. Don't fill it.
2. **Serifs for headlines, sans for body, mono for numbers.** Numbers ARE the content of a finance app; let them carry typographic weight.
3. **One accent color, shared across light + dark modes.** Brand identity must survive theme switches. The current sky-blue→purple flip is two products visually.
4. **No category-reflex palette.** Avoid navy+gold (Bloomberg), green+red (gain/loss IS necessary but as semantic accent, not brand), neon (crypto).
5. **One signature interaction, not twelve micro-interactions.** Pros use motion sparingly.
6. **Honest copy.** No fake testimonials, no fake metrics, no "join 50K+ traders." Every claim true or absent.

## Out of scope
- Marketing pages beyond Homepage
- Onboarding flows
- Settings, billing, pricing — this is not a SaaS
- A11y audit beyond WCAG-AA contrast on tokens

## Success criteria
A recruiter spending 30 seconds on the Homepage thinks "this person can design" before they think "this is a stock app." If they then scroll, the case study (README/About) closes the loop.
